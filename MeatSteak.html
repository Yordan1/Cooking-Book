Meat Steak

Ingredients:

  1. 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  2. 1 tablespoon butter
  3. 1 pound small carrots (about 5 inches long),
    halved lengthwise
  4. 1 pound turnips (about 3 medium),
    peeled and cut into thick matchsticks
  5. ¾ teaspoon salt, divided
  6. ¾ teaspoon ground pepper, divided
  7. 1 pound sirloin or top round steak, about 1 inch thick, trimmed
  8. 1 teaspoon minced fresh rosemary or ½ teaspoon dried
  9. 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  10. 1 tablespoon red-wine vinegar

Method:

Preheat oven to 450°F. Heat 1 tablespoon oil and butter in
a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
Add carrots and turnips, sprinkle with ¼ teaspoon each
salt and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally,
until starting to brown and soften, 8 to 10 minutes.
Transfer to a plate. Meanwhile, cut steak in
half crosswise and sprinkle with rosemary and
the remaining ½ teaspoon each salt and pepper.
Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon oil in the pan over
medium-high heat until very hot.
Add the steak and cook
until starting to brown on each side, about 2 minutes total.
Transfer to another plate. Return the vegetables to
the pan and stir in brown sugar. Place the steak
on the vegetables. Carefully transfer the pan to the oven.
Roast until the vegetables are tender and
steak is cooked to your desired doneness, 8 to 10
minutes for medium. Remove the steak to a clean
cutting board and let rest 5 minutes before slicing.
Drizzle vinegar over the vegetables. Serve the steak with the vegetables.
